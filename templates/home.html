Understanding Key Environmental Metrics

Mean Species Abundance (MSA)

MSA measures biodiversity intactness by comparing species abundance in disturbed versus pristine ecosystems. A value of 1.0 indicates complete intactness, while values approaching zero signify severe ecological degradation.

CO₂ Emissions

Carbon dioxide emissions are measured in tonnes and indicate the environmental impact of various industrial activities. The platform provides sector-specific emissions data to help identify areas needing improvement.

ESRS E4 Compliance

European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS) E4 focuses specifically on biodiversity and ecosystems reporting requirements. This standard helps companies report transparently on their biodiversity impacts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Mean Species Abundance (MSA) calculated?

MSA values are calculated using ecological models that compare current species abundance in a location with the abundance expected in a pristine, undisturbed reference state. Factors considered include land use, infrastructure, fragmentation, and pollution levels.

Where does the emissions data come from?

The CO₂ emissions data is compiled from Germany's Federal Environmental Agency (Umweltbundesamt), industrial reports, and sustainability disclosures from major companies operating within Germany, updated quarterly.

Can I export data from the platform?

Currently, direct data export functionality is in development. For now, you can use the reports feature to view visual representations of the data and take screenshots as needed.

How often is the data updated?

Environmental data is updated quarterly, with the most recent update being April 2025. All timestamps for specific datasets can be found in the information panel of each report.
